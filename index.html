WHAT IS LUPUS?

Understanding the condition

Lupus is a chronic autoimmune disease in which the immune system attacks thebody's own healthy tissue. It can affect the skin, joints, kidneys, heart, lungs, and brain — often mimicking other conditions, making early diagnosis difficult.

Understanding lupus is the first step towards living well with it. In Kenya and across Africa, awareness can significantly shorten the path to diagnosis and proper care.

5M+ People worldwide living with lupus
90% Of people diagnosed are women
6 Years Average diagnosis delay in Africa

Common Symptoms

  • Persistent fatigue and weakness
  • Joint pain, stiffness, and swelling
  • Butterfly-shaped rash on the face
  • Sensitivity to sunlight
  • Fever and frequent infections
  • Chest pain and hair loss

Our Mission & Vision

We believe that no one should navigate lupus alone. Our work is rooted in community, cultural understanding, and the pursuit of equitable healthcare.

Our Mission

To build a compassionate, accessible support ecosystem for Kenyans living with lupus and their caregivers—providing education, emotional support, and practical tools that honor our local context and strengthen the patient journey from suspicion to management.

Our Vision

A Kenya where lupus is widely recognized, rapidly diagnosed, and effectively managed. Where every patient feels empowered, heard, and surrounded by a community that sees their strength beyond the diagnosis.

Compassion

We meet every member with empathy, patience, and an unwavering commitment to emotional safety.

Inclusion

We embrace diverse experiences across age, gender, region, and caregiving role—every voice matters.

Empowerment

We equip our community with knowledge and confidence to ask questions, seek care, and thrive daily.
